The Canadian LLP vs. Wealth Advisor

See the invisible cost of pulling money out of your RRSP to fund school — even if you pay every cent back on time, the lost years of compounding never come back.

How to use this tool

Pick the education path that best matches your plan. The tool projects 25 years into the future and shows two side-by-side outcomes: the value your RRSP would have reached if you'd left it alone, vs. the value it actually reaches when you pull money out under the LLP and repay it perfectly over 10 years.

Use the toggles to test different growth assumptions and see whether the CRA's grace period before repayment makes the gap better or worse.

About the LLP (2026 CRA rules)

Limits: Up to $10,000 per year, with a $20,000 lifetime ceiling per LLP student.

Repayment: You repay the full balance in equal annual installments over 10 years, designated on Schedule 7. These repayments do not generate a tax deduction — you're simply restoring your own RRSP room.

Always confirm your room and repayment schedule in CRA My Account before moving money.

Legal disclaimer & assumptions

The Lifelong Learning Plan (LLP) is administered by the Canada Revenue Agency under the Income Tax Act. Withdrawal limits, repayment schedules, and qualifying-program rules are subject to legislative change. The figures shown here are illustrative projections only — they assume a constant annual growth rate, perfectly-timed repayments, and the simplified grace-period model described below.

Grace period model: When "CRA grace period" is on, repayments begin five years after the last withdrawal year. In practice the CRA rule is the earlier of (a) the fifth year after your first LLP withdrawal or (b) the second year after you cease to qualify as a full-time student — so your actual schedule may begin sooner. Toggle the grace period off to model immediate-repayment behaviour.

Tax treatment: LLP repayments are designated on Schedule 7 of your T1 return and do not generate a new RRSP deduction.

This tool is not tax, legal, or investment advice. Verify your LLP balance, available room, and repayment schedule in CRA My Account, and consult a qualified professional before acting.
