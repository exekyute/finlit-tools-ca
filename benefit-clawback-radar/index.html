The Canadian Benefit Clawback Radar

Earning your next dollar isn't always worth a dollar. See exactly where Canada's income tax + non-taxable benefit clawbacks combine into a steep, hidden tax on low and middle incomes.

How to use this tool

Pick a household situation and an income bracket below. The chart, pipeline, and summary cards all update together to show how much of your next dollar earned actually lands in your pocket — after federal and provincial income tax, and after the Canada Child Benefit or GIS gets quietly reduced behind the scenes.

Why these clawbacks matter

1. They're invisible on your paystub. CCB and GIS reductions show up months later through the CRA — not in your withholding. A raise can quietly cost more than it adds.

2. They stack with regular tax. A 20.5% federal bracket plus a 9.15% provincial bracket plus a 13.5% CCB phase-out is a real effective marginal rate of ~43% on the next dollar earned.

Notice on Benefit Indexation

CCB base amounts and phase-out thresholds are indexed to inflation annually every July. GIS amounts are updated quarterly by Service Canada. This visualization uses the 2026 parameter frameworks. A minor change in your Line 23600 Net Income can cause substantial variance in actual government distributions.
