The Canadian Benefit Clawback Radar

Earning your next dollar isn't always worth a dollar. See exactly where Canada's income tax + non-taxable benefit clawbacks combine into a steep, hidden tax on low and middle incomes.

How to use this tool

Pick a household situation, dial in your actual income and family size, and the chart, pipeline, and summary cards all update together. Then try the RRSP Escape Hatch at the bottom to see how a single deduction can flatten the cliff.

Why these clawbacks matter

1. They're invisible on your paystub. CCB and GIS reductions show up months later through the CRA — not in your withholding. A raise can quietly cost more than it adds.

2. They stack with regular tax. A 20.5% federal bracket plus a 9.15% provincial bracket plus a 13.5% CCB phase-out is a real effective marginal rate of ~43% on the next dollar earned.

Notice on Benefit Indexation

CCB base amounts and phase-out thresholds are indexed to inflation annually every July. GIS amounts are updated quarterly by Service Canada. This visualization uses the 2026 parameter frameworks. A minor change in your Line 23600 Net Income can cause substantial variance in actual government distributions.

Disclaimer & sources

This tool is illustrative only. It uses 2026-indexed estimates of CCB, GIS, and federal/provincial brackets for six provinces (ON, BC, AB, QC, NS, MB) and does not model provincial low-income credits (e.g. Ontario LIFT), health premiums or surtaxes (e.g. Ontario Health Premium), Quebec-specific deductions (QPP, QPIP, abatement), shared-custody splits, the GST/HST credit, provincial child benefits, the disability tax credit, or the childcare expense deduction.

RRSP contribution room is uncapped in the input — your real room depends on prior-year income. Verify your numbers in CRA My Account and Service Canada, and consult a Certified Professional Accountant before making employment or income decisions.
